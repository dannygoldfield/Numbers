This FAQ answers common questions about Numbers.
It does not define system behavior.

---

## Do I really own a number?

Numbers proceeds **as if** a number can be owned.

What that means is intentionally unresolved.
The system records outcomes.
Interpretation is left to the holder.

Ownership is recorded, not explained.

---

## Is this an Ordinals project?

Numbers uses Ordinals to record inscriptions on Bitcoin.

It does not extend the Ordinals protocol
or introduce new token mechanics.

Numbers uses existing primitives
without adding new ones.

---

## Why numbers?

Numbers are familiar, abstract, and culturally loaded.

They carry meaning without explanation.
That tension is the point.

---

## Is this serious?

Yes.

It is also simple, literal, and slightly strange.
Those qualities are intentional.

---

## Who is this for?

Anyone can observe the sequence.

Participation requires no belief beyond curiosity.
Some people will care more than others.

The system does not try to equalize interest.

---

## Why no rarity, traits, or visuals?

Numbers removes features instead of accumulating them.

The number is enough.

Any meaning comes from outside the system.

---

## What happens if nobody bids?

The sequence advances.

A number resolving without a bidder
is a valid and final outcome.

Not every number needs an owner to exist.

---

## Is this about speculation?

Numbers does not optimize for trading, liquidity, or price discovery.

People may speculate anyway.
The system does not respond to that.

---

## Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin provides a durable, public record.

Numbers relies on Bitcoin for finality,
not for narrative or symbolism.

Bitcoin is infrastructure, not content.

---

## Is this finished?

The rules are fixed.

The sequence continues.
